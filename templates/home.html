Input Quantum System Parameters

Gate Fidelity

Range: 0.900 - 0.999
Gate fidelity measures the accuracy of quantum gate operations. Higher values indicate more precise quantum operations with fewer errors. It represents how close the actual quantum gate operation is to the ideal theoretical operation.

Coherence Time (Î¼s)

Range: 10 - 100 microseconds
Coherence time is the duration a qubit maintains its quantum state before decoherence occurs. Longer coherence times allow for more complex quantum computations and reduced error rates.

Temperature (mK)

Range: 10 - 50 millikelvin
Operating temperature of the quantum processor. Lower temperatures reduce thermal noise and improve quantum state stability. Quantum computers typically operate near absolute zero.

Gate Count

Range: 10 - 200 gates
Total number of quantum gate operations in the circuit. More gates increase computational complexity but also accumulate more errors. Each gate operation introduces a small probability of error.

Circuit Depth

Range: 5 - 50 layers
Circuit depth represents the number of sequential gate layers in a quantum circuit. Deeper circuits can perform more complex computations but are more susceptible to accumulated errors over time.

Qubit Connectivity

Range: 0.500 - 1.000
Measures how well qubits are interconnected in the quantum processor. Higher connectivity enables more efficient quantum algorithms and reduces the need for additional SWAP gates.

Readout Fidelity

Range: 0.800 - 0.980
Accuracy of measuring the final quantum state. High readout fidelity ensures that measurement results correctly represent the quantum computation outcome without introducing measurement errors.

Crosstalk Level

Range: 0.010 - 0.100
Measures unwanted interaction between neighboring qubits. Lower crosstalk levels indicate better qubit isolation and reduced interference, leading to more accurate quantum operations.
